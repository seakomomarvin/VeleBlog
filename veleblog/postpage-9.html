Kumasi Kejetia Market has no insurance cover- Petty Traders Chairman

Kumasi Kejetia Market has no insurance cover

Petty Traders Association's Chairman Nana Prempeh has disclosed that since November 2021, the ultra-mordern Kejetia-market has not been insured. He also makes allegations of the facility not having a valid fire station in the structure.

Speaking on the radio, the leader of traders warned that incase of any incidence, traders and occupants of the facility can lose their investment. On the radio statio he stated that "We all wish well for the kejetia market but it seems some people are against this. It is our wish that the market florishes so that people will come buy goods and services but we see that some people don't want it the way".

"Kojo, I'm telling you, that the Kejetia Market has no insurance cover now. We have been paying insurance of a little above GHC 51.00, but the managers of the faclity have failed to renew the place", he revealed.

The traders are unhappy with the Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly(KMA) and the Board and Managers of the Kejetia Market as they claim their only interest is to milk traders dry. They are therefore calling for a proper accountability and management of the facility.

The Kumasi Metropolitan Assembly has not been available for comment
